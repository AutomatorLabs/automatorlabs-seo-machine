# Business Context

## What The Business Appears To Be Doing

AutomatorLabs is building an organic search and newsletter acquisition engine around personal finance decision support.

The product shape is:

- free calculators for specific intents
- educational guides for adjacent and higher-funnel queries
- worked examples for long-tail search coverage
- newsletter capture as the retained audience channel

## Audience

Based on the content and copy, the primary audience appears to be:

- consumers researching personal finance decisions
- beginners who want plain-English explanations
- search visitors with concrete scenario questions
- users comparing options in investing, retirement, debt, mortgages, and savings

## Core Value Proposition

The repo consistently reinforces a few promises:

- calculators should explain the result, not just output a number
- users should be able to test assumptions
- content should help users move from question to understanding
- examples should make abstract formulas concrete

## Acquisition Strategy

The visible acquisition engine is SEO plus newsletter capture.

SEO acquisition comes from:

- calculator intent pages
- category pages
- guide hubs
- topical guides
- comparison guides
- programmatic example clusters
- dense internal linking across those surfaces

Retention and audience capture come from:

- `/newsletter/`
- shared Beehiiv CTA placements across calculators, guides, examples, and hubs

## Monetization

Stated intent (from project owner, not yet implemented in source): newsletter growth, affiliate products, sponsorships, future premium tools, and future SaaS products.

The inspected repo does not yet show any of these in code:

- subscriptions
- checkout
- ads
- affiliate systems
- lead forms beyond newsletter signup

Current model is audience growth first, monetization later. Build decisions should keep long-term SEO durability and authority ahead of short-term monetization hacks.

## Product Philosophy

The source strongly suggests these principles:

- education over hype
- planning over prediction
- scenario modeling over certainty
- breadth within a constrained financial domain
- quality-controlled programmatic SEO instead of volume-first page factories

## Implications For Contributors

- Every new page should strengthen the acquisition graph, not exist in isolation.
- "More pages" is not enough; each page must fit the calculator-guide-example system.
- Internal links and topical adjacency are part of the business model.
- Newsletter CTAs are part of the conversion path and should not be removed casually.
